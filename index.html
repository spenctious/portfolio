More about me

From the moment I built my first computer from a kit and powered it up I was hooked. I've not always been a professional developer but I always seem drawn back to coding's unique blend of creativity, problem-solving and ability to make cool things happen.

After five years being a developer my career started to veer off into analysis. Unfortunately, while some of the roles were good, overall they never really added up, aligned or built into anything meaningful. I had become a generalist and ended up with the completely unsellable, unfixable CV to match.

So now I'm pressing the reset button and looking to get back into development. It means starting again but I have to say I'm really rather enjoying the challenge.

As for life outside of work, these have been some of my more time-consuming interests:

Travel

Big challenging trips. For the first trip I set off alone to hitch-hike through Africa, later trips were overland from UK to New Zealand and Central Asia by bike along the Pamir Highway.

Teaching

I found volunteer teaching in an Indian primary school so rewarding I once almost considered it as an alternative career. I still keep in touch with my old school and maintain an interest in the subject.

Publishing

I've made hardback books of my travels and published a guidebook to walking on the Canary Island of La Palma (yes, the one that recently erupted).

Hiking

I became a convert after walking in South Africa which blew me away with its beauty. Since then I've hiked extensively in Africa, Europe, Canada (the wrong bit), Asia and of course here in the UK.
